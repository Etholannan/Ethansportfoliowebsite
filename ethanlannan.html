An article on my applications:

Audio Culture:

Audio culture is something you may have heard of before and something you have definitely been a part of. Podcasts, audiobooks, and the plethora of new audio-based platforms are leading the way when it comes to spreading knowledge, entertainment, and genuine conversation. The movement toward audio-based content has been gaining huge momentum in the last 5 years. Audio blogs came first originating in the 1980s with the advent of broadband internet access and portable digital audio playback devices, which gradually transitioned into the vast podcasting world we have today. Users truly didnt start utilizing audio content until podcasting started surpassing radio and became a mainstream form of entertainment around 2008. Audio culture now is a robust platform led by thousands of powerful creators. While audio culture is a massive platform with huge contributors, it is very one. In the audio culture world you're either a creator or a listener and the means to becoming a creator can be pretty tough compared to other forms of media. I saw a need for a way in for everyone, and with Sonar I wanted to bridge the gap between active social media platforms and podcasting. Sonar is an experimental social network solely based on sound clips.

Sonar

Sonar is a social network that allows every user to easily become a creator in the audio culture world. With Sonar, users can create and contribute to communities through short-form audio clips. Users can spread real knowledge, genuine conversation, and entertainment in a way that they can't do elsewhere. The power behind a platform solely based on soundbites can be explained in many ways, but the most substantial example is the genuineness that comes with it. Users on platforms like Twitter hide behind a wall of text which allows them to communicate in ways they wouldn't normally use in real conversation. This can create an unhealthy and toxic environment that can be observed every day on other platforms and is commonly talked about. The audio form allows us to communicate in the way that we were made to, through real conversation. The other benefits that come with audio content are the deep emotions conveyed through speech that don't come with text, the multitasking ability, and the ease of censorship that comes with it. Creating this platform was a huge challenge for me as I am only a frontend developer and designer. Getting to a fully launched stage took much longer than it should've, but once we got the momentum going things started falling into place very fast.



Deivze

Devize is an application meant to harness the power of audio based content but in a different way. This application, while very new, is meant to bring audio culture to collaboration in the work place. Devize allows users to create teams where they can post and interact through audio-clips for brainstorming and conversation. Audio communication in the workplace creates a more genuine and natural environment which will increase productivity as a whole. At the point of writing this we are still prelaunch, but the hype and backing behind this application is at an all time high.



Where we are

At the time of writing this we are in the early stages of launch and bringing in our first user base. We are approaching the end of our pre-seed funding and are beginning to execute a marketing campaign while searching for seed round investors. Things have been quite interesting and exciting as we move through these first steps but I couldn't be happier with where we are at with this project.
